 📝 Project Overview

​This project focuses on a deep-dive analysis of customer behavior within an e-commerce platform. Using Python and advanced visualization libraries, I analyzed churn patterns to understand why customers leave and what factors contribute to long-term loyalty. This data-driven approach assists in designing strategic retention campaigns to minimize churn and maximize customer lifetime value.
​🔑 Key Features

    ​Global Retention Status: Visualizing the health of the customer base with a clear 71.1% Active vs. 28.9% Churned ratio.
    ​Geographical Churn Distribution: Identifying churn hotspots across countries like USA, UK, France, and Canada.
    ​Demographic Insights: Analyzing churn probability based on customer age and gender-based loyalty metrics.
    ​Churn Probability Mapping: Utilizing distribution plots to identify specific age groups most prone to churn.

​🛠 Tech Stack

    ​Language: Python
    ​Libraries: Pandas (Data Manipulation), Matplotlib & Seaborn (Advanced Visualization).
    ​Environment: Jupyter Notebook / Kaggle for end-to-end analysis.

​💡 Key Insights

    ​Churn Rate: A 28.9% churn rate signals a critical need for targeted re-engagement strategies to boost retention.
    ​Market Trends: The USA shows the highest volume of both active and churned customers, making it a priority market.
    ​Gender-Based Loyalty: Analysis reveals distinct patterns in how male and female customers engage with the platform.
    ​Age Factor: Churn probability is most volatile in mid-age segments, suggesting that changing lifestyle needs may influence retention.
